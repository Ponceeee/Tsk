Background of the Company

Sterling Bank of Asia (SBA) is a savings bank in the Philippines, established on March 30, 2007, in response to the government's mandate to create specialized financial institutions that would support the development and growth of the small and medium enterprise (SME) sector. The bank is principally owned by the JTKC Group of Companies, Surewell Equities, and Star Equities, which are engaged in diverse industries ranging from logistics to finance, real estate, manufacturing, and hotel and resort properties.

History

In 2009, SBA expanded its operations by acquiring Centennial Savings Bank, increasing its branch network and customer base. As of 2022, SBA operated 48 branches across the Philippines and was ranked as the sixth-largest thrift bank in the country in terms of total assets.

Corporate Profile

Sterling Bank of Asia, Inc. (A Savings Bank) is a privately held savings bank established on March 30, 2007, regulated by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP). The bank was founded in response to the Philippine government's initiative to support the development and growth of the small and medium enterprise (SME) sector. SBA offers a comprehensive range of financial products and services tailored to SMEs, corporations, and individual clients, including deposits, loans, investments, asset management, and other banking services.

Ownership and Affiliations

Sterling Bank of Asia is principally owned by the JTKC Group of Companies, Surewell Equities, and Star Equities. These entities are involved in diverse industries such as logistics, finance, real estate, manufacturing, and hospitality.
