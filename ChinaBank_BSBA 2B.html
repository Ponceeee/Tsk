Background & History

1920

Foundation

China Banking Corporation (China Bank) was established as the first privately owned local commercial bank in the Philippines. It played a key role in serving the financial needs of Chinese-Filipino businessmen during a time when ethnic Chinese had limited access to banking facilities.

1940s

Surviving WWII

The bank survived World War II and continued its operations, showing remarkable resilience during a challenging period for the Philippines.

1960s-1990s

Growth and Expansion

China Bank experienced significant growth, expanding its branch network and service offerings to become one of the most stable financial institutions in the Philippines.

2020

Centennial Celebration

China Bank marked its centennial, representing 100 years of banking excellence and service to the Philippine economy.

Corporate Profile

China Banking Corporation (PSE stock symbol: CHIB) is one of the leading private universal banks in the Philippines.

It provides a wide range of banking products and services to corporate, commercial, and retail customers through 637 branches nationwide, complemented by its digital banking and payment platforms.

Capital Structure and Adequacy

Capital Structure

China Banking Corporation maintains a strong capital position that exceeds regulatory requirements and supports its growth strategies. The bank's capital structure consists primarily of:

  • Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) Capital
  • Additional Tier 1 (AT1) Capital
  • Tier 2 Capital

Capital Adequacy Ratios (as of 2022)

  • Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) Ratio: 14.01%
  • Tier 1 Capital Ratio: 14.01%
  • Total Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR): 15.03%

These ratios significantly exceed the BSP's minimum requirements of 6.0%, 7.5%, and 10.0% for CET1, Tier 1, and Total CAR, respectively.

Dividend Policy

China Bank implements a dividend policy that balances the objectives of providing shareholders with competitive returns and maintaining sufficient capital for reinvestment and growth opportunities.

Key Developments by Year

2023

  • Record Profit: Chinabank achieved a net income of ₱22.0 billion, marking a 15% increase from the previous year.
  • Loan Growth: Gross loans expanded by 10% to ₱791 billion, with consumer loans comprising 23% of the portfolio.
  • Asset Quality: Maintained a non-performing loan (NPL) ratio of 2.5% and NPL coverage at 104%.
  • Digital Transformation: Invested significantly in IT infrastructure as part of ongoing digital initiatives.
  • Recognition: Received the Five-Golden Arrow award from the Institute of Corporate Directors and was named 2023 Employer of the Year by the People Management Association of the Philippines.

2022

  • Sustained Growth: Net income rose to ₱19.1 billion, a 26% increase year-over-year.
  • Operational Efficiency: Improved cost-to-income ratio through prudent expense management.
