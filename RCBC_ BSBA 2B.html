Background & History

Rizal Commercial Banking Corporation (RCBC) was established on September 23, 1960, as Rizal Development Bank, a small development bank in Rizal province. In 1963, it received approval from the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas to operate as a commercial bank and began operations under its present name, Rizal Commercial Banking Corporation.

Founding Era (1960s)

RCBC established its first head office at 219 Buendia Avenue in Makati (then part of Rizal), making it the first commercial bank to do so in the province. The bank was acquired by members of the Yuchengco Group of Companies (YGC) in 1962.

Growth & Expansion (1980s-2000s)

In 1989, RCBC received its universal banking license. Through the years, it grew through strategic acquisitions and organic growth. In 2000, it acquired major control of Bankard Inc., a credit card company. In 2007, RCBC acquired Merchants Savings and Loan Association Inc., consolidating it with RCBC Savings Bank to increase its branch network.

Modern Era (2010s-Present)

In 2019, the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas approved RCBC's merger with its thrift bank RCBC Savings Bank. Today, RCBC is the 5th largest privately owned universal bank in the Philippines, with over ₱1.3 trillion in total resources as of June 2024.

Corporate Profile

Rizal Commercial Banking Corporation (RCBC) is one of the leading universal banks in the Philippines with total consolidated resources of over ₱1.3 trillion (as of June 30, 2024). It is majority-owned by the Yuchengco Group of Companies (YGC), one of the oldest and largest conglomerates in Southeast Asia.

Other significant investors of the bank include the World Bank's International Finance Corporation, Cathay Life Insurance Co., Ltd., a wholly owned subsidiary of Cathay Financial Holding, Ltd., and Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation (SMBC).

RCBC Subsidiaries

  • RCBC Capital Corporation - Investment banking arm
  • RCBC Securities, Inc. - Stock brokerage
  • RCBC Bankard Services Corporation - Credit card services
  • RCBC Forex Brokers Corporation - Foreign exchange brokerage
  • RCBC International Finance Limited - International finance
  • Rizal Microbank, Inc. - Microfinance operations
  • RCBC Leasing and Finance Corporation - Leasing and financing

Branches and Offices

RCBC has an extensive network of delivery channels to better serve its growing customer base. As of December 31, 2024, it has:

  • 465 branches nationwide
  • 1,482 ATMs
  • 8,426 ATM Go terminals with presence across all 82 provinces of the country

This network is complemented by RCBC's digital channels including Internet banking and mobile banking applications for both iOS and Android platforms.

Scope of Business/Operation

As a universal bank, RCBC offers a wide range of banking and financial services to various customer segments:

Consumer Banking

Providing retail banking products and services to individual customers, including savings and checking accounts, personal loans, mortgages, credit cards, and digital banking services.

Corporate Banking

Offering specialized services to large corporations and business conglomerates, including corporate loans, cash management solutions, trade services, and investment banking.

Commercial Banking

Serving medium-sized businesses with tailored financial solutions, including commercial loans, deposit products, and trade finance services.

SME Banking

Providing financial services to small and medium enterprises through specialized lending programs, deposit products, and cash management services.

Microfinance

Through Rizal Microbank, serving microenterprises and small business operators, as well as value chain players particularly in the agricultural sector.

Digital Banking

Pioneering digital banking solutions like DiskarTech, RCBC's financial inclusion "super app," and other online banking platforms to provide convenient access to banking services.

Investment Banking

Through RCBC Capital Corporation, providing services such as underwriting, project finance, and corporate financial advisory.

Treasury

Managing the bank's liquidity and investment portfolio, and offering treasury products to clients including foreign exchange and fixed-income securities.

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

RCBC is committed to contributing to social development and sustainability through various corporate social responsibility initiatives:

Sustainable Finance

RCBC is a pioneer in sustainable finance in the Philippines, having issued the country's first green bond under the ASEAN Green Bond Standards. The bank raised ₱15 billion through this initiative to finance environmental and climate-friendly projects.

Environmental Sustainable Finance

Financial Inclusion

Through its DiskarTech app, RCBC reaches unbanked and underbanked Filipinos, providing accessible financial services to those in remote areas and grassroots communities. The app has recorded over 5.4 million downloads, reflecting its impact on financial inclusion.

Financial Inclusion Digital Banking

Barangayan Banking

RCBC's ATM Go program brings banking services to far-flung communities through portable ATM terminals, with 8,426 terminals deployed across all 82 provinces in the Philippines. This initiative helps government beneficiaries access financial aid and services without traveling to urban centers.

Financial Inclusion Community Development

Environmental Sustainability

RCBC has implemented policies to reduce its environmental footprint, including energy-efficient practices in its offices and branches. The bank also promotes paperless banking through its digital channels to conserve resources.

Environmental Sustainability
